Welcome to My Engineering Portfolio

My name is Jacky Riven, and I’m currently pursuing my degree in Computer Engineering at the University of Alaska Fairbanks. This portfolio showcases a selection of my projects, ranging from digital logic design and embedded systems to analog circuit development and software engineering.

This website is structured to present not only the functionality of each project, but also to explain the design process behind them, the challenges I encountered, and the skills I applied and developed along the way. Each section is crafted to be accessible to both engineers and curious viewers interested in the behind-the-scenes of how systems work.

The projects you’ll find here include a maze-based game written in C++ using a stack navigation system, a three-stage analog amplifier designed from scratch, and a modular 16-bit MIPS processor constructed using VHDL, featuring components like a register file and arithmetic logic unit (ALU).

I built this portfolio as a professional presentation tool to help communicate my work during job and internship applications. It’s also a personal reflection space—an organized timeline of my progress and a foundation for everything I’ll continue to build.
